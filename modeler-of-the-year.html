TPG Modeler of the Year Trophy

Each year, the Torrey Pines Gulls honor one or more modelers who have demonstrated levels of volunteerism or flying achievement that separates them from the rest with the Modeler of the Year trophy. This trophy was donated by Mark Smith to the club in 1971. Mark Smith was the operator of Mark’s Models, maker of the popular Windward, Windfree, Wanderer and other famous kits. Officially the trophy is called the “Mark’s Models Perpetual Award for Outstanding Member in the Torrey Pines Gulls” but over time it has been shortened to “Modeler of the Year.” Mark was a wonderful modeler who enjoyed flying at Torrey Pines and other locations in southern California with his father Rod Smith. He was an amazing asset to the hobby and a fierce competitor in the early years of competitive RC soaring, and a true friend of the Torrey Pines Gulls.

Mark’s legacy of club involvement lives on through this trophy and the excellent effort made on behalf of the Torrey Pines Gulls by the many winners listed below.
